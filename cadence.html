* About Cadence

Cadence was born from a conviction: that cadets have something to say, and the world should hear it. As Founding Editor, I built a publication that wasn't just a school magazine — it was a platform for young Bangladeshi voices to engage with literature, history, culture, and the questions that don't appear on any syllabus.


The name carries its meaning deliberately. Cadence — rhythm, pace, the beat that holds a march together. A magazine, too, is a rhythm. A cadence of ideas.

* The Idea
"A cadet is not just a soldier in training. A cadet is a mind in formation."

When I started Cadence, the prevailing idea was that a cadet's job was discipline, order, duty. All true. But discipline without reflection produces obedience, not leadership. Order without questioning produces compliance, not wisdom.

Cadence was built on the belief that the best leaders are also readers, writers, thinkers — people who have wrestled with ideas and come out the other side with something worth saying. The magazine gave cadets a space to do that wrestling in public.

Every issue asks: what are we curious about? What troubles us? What makes us proud or ashamed or uncertain? These are not soft questions. They are the questions that define what we stand for.
